## Welcome to Content Management

The MMS PPG website uses **Directus CMS**, a powerful and user-friendly content management system. This guide will help you understand the basics of creating, editing, and publishing content.

### Content List View

<img src="/images/content/content-list-view.png" alt="Content list showing blog posts with titles, status, and dates" />

When you click on a content type (like Blog Posts), you'll see:

- **Search Bar**: Find specific content quickly
- **Filters**: Filter by status, author, date, etc.
- **Sort Options**: Order by date, title, or other fields
- **Bulk Actions**: Select multiple items for batch operations
- **Create Button**: Add new content

### Content Edit View

<img src="/images/content/content-edit-view.png" alt="Content editing interface with form fields and rich text editor" />

When editing content, you'll see:

- **Form Fields**: Input areas for title, content, etc.
- **Rich Text Editor**: Format your content with styling tools
- **Media Picker**: Select and upload images
- **Status Controls**: Set draft, review, or published status
- **Save Options**: Save draft or publish immediately

## Content Workflow

Understanding the content workflow helps ensure quality and consistency:

<img src="/images/content/workflow-diagram.png" alt="Content workflow diagram showing draft, review, and published stages" />

### Workflow Stages

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Draft">
    **What it means**: Content is being created or edited
    
    **Who can see it**: Only the creator and administrators
    
    **What you can do**:
    - Make unlimited changes
    - Save your progress
    - Preview how it will look
    - Take your time to perfect it
    
    **When to use**: While you're still working on the content
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="In Review">
    **What it means**: Content is ready for review and approval
    
    **Who can see it**: Creator, reviewers, and administrators
    
    **What happens**:
    - Reviewers get notified automatically
    - They can add comments and suggestions
    - Content remains private until approved
    - You may receive feedback to address
    
    **When to use**: When you're happy with your content and ready for feedback
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Published">
    **What it means**: Content is live on the website
    
    **Who can see it**: Everyone visiting the website
    
    **What happens**:
    - Content appears on the public website
    - Search engines can find it
    - Visitors can read and share it
    - Analytics start tracking views
    
    **When to use**: When content is approved and ready for the public
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Best Practices

### Writing Guidelines

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="âœï¸ Clear and Simple Language">
    - Use everyday language that everyone can understand
    - Avoid medical jargon unless necessary (and explain it)
    - Keep sentences short and to the point
    - Use active voice ("We provide services" not "Services are provided")
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="ðŸ“– Structure Your Content">
    - Start with the most important information
    - Use headings to break up long text
    - Include bullet points and numbered lists
    - Add a clear call-to-action at the end
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="ðŸŽ¯ Know Your Audience">
    - Write for patients and community members
    - Consider different levels of health literacy
    - Be empathetic and supportive in tone
    - Include practical, actionable information
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="ðŸ“± Mobile-Friendly Content">
    - Keep paragraphs short (2-3 sentences)
    - Use descriptive headings
    - Ensure images display well on small screens
    - Test how your content looks on mobile
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Image Guidelines

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Image Quality" icon="image">
    **Resolution**: At least 1200px wide for featured images
    
    **Format**: JPG for photos, PNG for graphics with text
    
    **Size**: Keep file sizes under 2MB for faster loading
    
    **Composition**: Use high-quality, professional images
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Accessibility" icon="universal-access">
    **Alt Text**: Always describe what's in the image
    
    **Context**: Explain why the image is relevant
    
    **Decorative**: Mark purely decorative images appropriately
    
    **Text in Images**: Avoid text in images when possible
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### SEO Basics

<Steps>
  <Step title="Write Descriptive Titles">
    - Keep titles under 60 characters
    - Include relevant keywords naturally
    - Make them compelling and clickable
    - Avoid keyword stuffing
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Create Meta Descriptions">
    - Summarize the content in 150-160 characters
    - Include a call-to-action
    - Make it enticing to click
    - Use natural language
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Use Clean URLs">
    - Keep URLs short and descriptive
    - Use hyphens to separate words
    - Avoid special characters
    - Include relevant keywords
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Structure with Headings">
    - Use H1 for the main title (automatic)
    - Use H2 for main sections
    - Use H3 for subsections
    - Keep hierarchy logical
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common Tasks

### Creating New Content

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose Content Type">
    Navigate to the appropriate section (Blog Posts, Events, etc.)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click Create Item">
    Look for the "+" or "Create Item" button
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Fill Required Fields">
    Complete all fields marked with a red asterisk (*)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Optional Content">
    Include images, categories, and other relevant information
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save as Draft">
    Save your work regularly to avoid losing changes
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review and Publish">
    When ready, change status to "In Review" or "Published"
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Editing Existing Content

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the Content">
    Use the search bar or browse the content list
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click to Edit">
    Click on the content title or the edit icon
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Make Changes">
    Edit any fields as needed
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save Changes">
    Click "Save" to preserve your edits
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Managing Images

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Files Section">
    Click on "Files" in the main navigation
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Upload New Images">
    Drag and drop files or click "Upload Files"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Alt Text">
    Click on an image and add descriptive alt text
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Organize in Folders">
    Create folders to keep images organized
  </Step>
</Steps>
